Summary

  • The Cannon is a single-target defense that deals moderate damage. It is the first defensive structure that a player builds at the start of the game. The player is required to build one during the tutorial.
  • Cannons are both cheap and quick to upgrade at lower levels.
  • Cannons can shoot any ground unit, such as Barbarians and Archers, but it cannot shoot any air units, such as Dragons, Balloons, etc.
  • Starting from level 7, which requires Town Hall 6, a Cannon can be geared up by the Master Builder if he is available and at least one Double Cannon in the Builder Base is upgraded to level 4, which requires Builder Hall 4. This gives the Cannon a "Burst" mode, which doubles its damage output by shooting a burst of four cannonballs that deals 36% more damage but range is shorter two tiles shorter, the main cooldown is half the normal attack rate on top of a short delays for each burst shot after the first.
  • At Town Hall 16, two level 21 Cannons that are not geared up can be merged to form a Ricochet Cannon, and at Town Hall 17, the geared-up level 21 Cannon and Archer Tower can be merged to form the Multi-Gear Tower.

Strategies

Defensive Strategies

  • Cannons have a high rate of fire and a decent range, which makes them an ideal deterrent for Wall Breakers (which have very low hitpoints). They can also pick off Archers or Wizards that are used in an attempt to set a funnel.
  • Cannons are very useful for keeping Giants from attacking other defenses.
  • You can defend Mortars and Wizard Towers by placing them within range of Cannons.
  • Make sure Cannons are placed within range of Air Defenses, because Cannons are unable to target air units (or at least in range of an Archer Tower or Wizard Tower which can also target air units).
    • They can also synergize well with Air Defenses specifically.
  • Upgrade these structures and Mortars first at lower levels, as they are one of your main ground defenses. They are also fairly cheap and are among the most common defensive structures available, which makes them a good initial investment for defense.
  • As the description implies, they are best used for point defenses, so putting them a bit outside of your base would be effective.
  • At Town Hall 5 (and above), if two Cannons are placed near each other, it could be a great help as sometimes players mostly try to go for the Archer Towers using Giants, which makes them shotguns thus taking out Giants easily.
    • However, this strategy is much less effective on tanks like the Barbarian King.
  • A geared up Cannon is highly ineffective against swarms of smaller troops due to its slow rate of fire. It may deal lots of damage, but most of its cannonball shots prove to be useless due to low-hitpoint troops dying in few hits and is easily overwhelmed, due to its sluggish retargeting time.
    • In contrast, however, Geared-Up Cannons are more effective against single-target high hitpoint troops. It can easily decimate medium troops, such as Giants, Hog Riders, Valkyries, Druids and Miners (if they are by themselves), and can deal tons of damage to Golems, Ice Golems, Root Riders and P.E.K.K.As. Because of this, geared-up cannons should be placed further within the base rather than outside to maximize their damage output. (This strategy is best for Town Halls 7 to 11).

Offensive Strategies

  • Cannons are one of the defensive buildings that cannot attack air units. This makes them easy pickings for Balloons, Dragons, or Minions if they are not within the range of Air Defenses, Archer Towers, X-Bows (on ground and air mode), Hidden Teslas, or Wizard Towers.
  • Cannons are only able to damage one unit at a time. This makes it very easy to overwhelm them with large groups of Archers or Barbarians. They can also be overwhelmed with higher health units such as Giants, Golems, Yetis, Ice Golems, or P.E.K.K.As.
  • Strong solo troops such as the Super Barbarian, P.E.K.K.A., or Valkyrie can destroy a Cannon even when surrounded by trash buildings if it has low support.

Upgrade Differences

  • When initially constructed, the Cannon consists of a short dark metal barrel mounted on a crude four-legged wooden rotational mount made of multiple planks on top of a circular metal plate.
  • At level 2, the whole Cannon is bigger. The wood of rotational mount is now one whole piece instead of multiple wooden planks, the legs are rotated 45 degrees and receives wooden stabilizers tied to each legs with ropes.
  • At level 3, the barrel is bigger and receives two decorative latitudinal ridges, one midway along the barrel and one at the rear. The wood of rotational mount is replaced with metal and bottom circular metal plate becomes bigger. The platform's leg stabilizer is removed in favor of a larger and stouter main support platform with the rotational mount is fitted inside, receives riveted metal plates on the sides to anchor the barrel more firmly to the rotational mount, and the wooden legs are replaced with shorter metal legs with a rivet.
  • At level 4, the top of rotational mount is reverted to wood again. The main support is polished, the riveted metal plates and legs are removed for a stone foundation.
  • At level 5, the barrel is bigger and used a darker metal, the decorative ridges are thicker and trimmed to be more pointed, one more is added around the muzzle.
  • At level 6, the rotational mount is raised, and the wooden base and stone foundation are discarded for a taller white stone platform with a pair of gold plate is added to the right edge of the platform and its opposing side, and four short gold-trimmed wooden legs.
  • At level 7, the barrel's decorative middle ridges is made of gold and the bottom rotational mount has a straight edge. There are two additional gold plates perpendicular to the first pair and they are all wider, the side of them also receive a steel bolt with a gold top.
  • At level 8, the rear decorative ridge also becomes gold.
  • At level 9, the muzzle decorative ridge also becomes gold. The bottom rotational mount also becomes gold and four golden spikes are added to the top of the base no occupied by the side gold plate. The gold trimming on the legs is much bigger and the wooden leg is replaced with dark metal with cone-shaped spike.
  • At level 10, the barrel is entirely made of gold and bigger. The main body of the barrel receives triangular reinforcing plates and the decorative ridge are flattened to be a band, with a wider one added to its neck.
  • At level 11, the barrel is transformed into black metal and it is made of lets of flat faces so it is no longer as circular. The triangular plates become fan-shaped and he neck decorative band used a lighter gray metal. The rotational mount and the gold spikes on top of the base also turns into black metal. The legs becomes prominent black crystal footings.
  • At level 12, the neck band is mostly trimmed down and replaced with a red hot ring behind the muzzle. The muzzle band changes from smooth to plated. The triangular plates glows orange under it because of the heat. The rotational mount and the spikes are removed, and gold plate on the base receives a dark red band. The footings are now some small black rocks surrounding a pointed crystal glowing a dull red which is brighter at the tip, similar to the level 4 X-Bow.
  • At level 13, the fan-shaped plates turn into more of a kite shape with a metal rectangle bar at the back, the orange outline now fully surrounds the plates, and a kite-shaped spike is added between the plates on the top of the barrel, which also merged the middle and back decorative bands. The barrel is raised. The neck decorative band now touches the back band and split into pieces that connects to the muzzle plates with strips, with flat gold spike on each plate around the muzzle. The dark red band on the base turns to a dark-gray and the white stone platform is replaced with material similar to the black crystal as it glows a dull red unevenly. The black rocks in the footings turn golden.
  • At level 14, the barrel is much wider. The triangular plates change to a trapezoidal shape and the rectangle bars becomes gold, the orange outline cover it too. The hemisphere at the back is also heated up to an orange color. The strips fastened with nails are now fused to the muzzle itself, there are less of them but are wider, the spikes become bigger, and the ring inside now glows orange as well. The inner muzzle uses a lighter gray metal color it glows a dim red. The base is now a lava pool, a white stone foundation surrounds it and gold linings on the foundation inner edge. Each corner has a cube with three golden spikes protruding.
  • At level 15, the barrel becomes octagonal shaped with two gray metal bands around the neck and muzzle of the barrel, with the neck one held in place with golden circular bolts and a small golden ring hanging from a fitting on each side. In between these two metal bands is a glowing yellow. The rear of the barrel is riveted with metallic strips replacing the trapezoidal plates, having a gold band around where the gold bar was. The muzzle itself is much larger. The base gains some black stone pieces on the corners replacing the spikes, the inner gold lining is replaced golden plates covering most of the remaining edge.
  • At level 16, the band around the muzzle is now even bigger and fused with the muzzle; The band with golden pentagonal bolts now is fully made of gold covering the yellow glow. The kite-shaped spike and the metallic strips are replaced by a metal band with spikes on each side, a thick metal plate run across the back from the top of the band. The base is no longer a lava pool, the rotational mount is visible again and is an octagonal black metal, the base is made of a two-step white stone, mostly covered with blue carpet and gold linings that almost forms an octagon by touching the hypotenuse of the corners while leaving a bit of the stairs visible. The corners are made of black metal in the shape of right-angle triangular prism which extends a bit more at the foundation, the top has a pentagonal bolt.
  • At level 17, the golden band now has big spike on each side and is wide enough to cover the slanted faces in the middle of the barrel, while the main body is now grooved rather than spiked, and thick metal plate and the back of the barrel is also golden. The gold details on the base now fully covers the stairs and even the blue carpet on the first step. The pentagonal bolt on the top of each corner is replaced the with a bigger golden pentagonal screw.
  • At level 18, the barrel uses deep green metal, at the back is a layer of purple metal then another layer of deep green metal. The golden kite-shaped spikes and grey metal strips returns, and the strips are much thicker and connect the main body and the neck, the front of the strip has a deep green spike on top and the back is gold, and the kite-shaped spike is covered with a layer of icy blue metal. The muzzle is covered with a golden band, under the band is a layer of purple metal and the inner muzzle is icy blue. The base sinks down and has a deep turquoise carpet with a ring of purple metal. The corner is also made of deep green metal with two steps, the top is a layer of icy blue metal, and their footing is similar to the strip on the barrel also having the deep green spike. The edges also have what looks like two connected level 5 gold Walls.
  • At level 19, the gold band around the muzzle extends a bit, with a thin layer of grey metal also added to the back. The strips on the main body has a layer gold covering most of it, including the spike, the front of the strip is trimmed to a point. The kite-shaped spikes are now a triangular plate which extends to the back while the gold underneath them is thicker extends more to the front. An icy blue metal band is added at the back of the barrel over the gold that is behind the strip, which connects to the icy blue metal plate. The gold has gotten shinier. The edge of the base has gold spikes and the footings are trapezoidal with a bigger spike.
  • At level 20, the body is now a green metal, with a layer of gold then a layer of purplish-black stone. The main body has a thick golden square wave pattern in a ring. The stone is carved in a way leaving a thin ring of gold band, the gold layer is also carved away leaving a triangle on top and showing the green metal layer; the stone on the neck have a triangle carved out letting green metal have the same pattern as the gold. The muzzle ring uses purplish-black stone instead of icy blue metal and shines green inside. The base is filled with greenish water, the rotational mount is made of purplish-black stone with a small footing on the side. The corners are also made of the purplish-black stone with a thin layer of foundation and gold linings starting from the edge, travels on top, then turn down at the corner. The edge looks like one level 3 Wall with a purple tint connecting to the corners.
  • At level 21, the body is now a purple metal. The main body alternates between a golden Town Hall 15 symbol at the top (upside-down hollow equilateral triangle) and a golden spike on a purple metal band, underneath is a wider gold band then dark purple metal. The muzzle is made of a gold band covered with a thinner dark purple metal band, the inner muzzle shines magenta. The base is mostly dark purple metal and the rotational mount is made of dark purple metal, with the small footing turns into a spike, the spike and the axle is now red. The gold linings on the corners are removed, a right-angle piece of gold is reinforced with dark purple metal plate on the side and a right-angle purple metal piece on top. The edge looks like triangle prism with an equilateral triangle plate on top.
  • The appearances of the cannonball change at certain levels.
    • When first constructed, it is light gray and extremely small.
    • At level 2, it becomes bigger.
    • At level 4, it becomes even bigger and darker.
    • At level 6, it gains spikes all over and is black.
    • At level 8, it glows a faint yellow.
    • At level 10, it is a dodecahedron with a small gap glowing yellow between each pentagonal pyramidal plates.
    • At level 12, the shape does not change but it is red hot with a layer of orange molten metal around it and leaving behind fire particles when traveling.
    • At level 13, it is a lot bigger, glowing in bright yellow, and surrounds by a lot more molten metal which creates much more fire particles.
    • At level 20, it spins, glows green, the molten metal and fire particles glow cyan, and the pentagonal pyramidal plates become much taller and golden, acting like giant spikes.
    • At level 21, it glows pink and the molten metal and particles glow orange again.
  • Gearing up the Cannon adds an additional, identical barrel alongside the original one.
    • At levels 7 to 12, the ridges which surround Cannons get extended and appear to surround both the Cannons.
    • At levels 13 and 14, the kite-shaped spike is place in between the Cannons. From now on, any notable protrusion around the back, neck, or muzzle will usually fuse into one ring.
    • At levels 15, 18, 19, and 21, instead of having the normal top feature in between the Cannons, it is replaced by a strip while each Cannon still has the normal feature on top.
    • However, the one exception is the level 20 Cannon, where the two cannons do not have any extra details.

Icon Descriptions

Tapping this icon displays information about the Cannon, such as Level, Damage Per Second, Hitpoints, Range, Damage Type, and Targets.
Tapping this icon begins upgrading the Cannon to the next level, if you have enough resources and a free Builder (or B.O.B). When the Cannon is at maximum level, this icon is not shown.
Tapping this icon instantly upgrades the Cannon to the next level, at the cost of one Hammer of Building if you have one. When the Cannon is at maximum level, or if the Town Hall requirements for the next level are not met, this icon is not shown.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, instantly finishes that upgrade at the cost of the displayed number of Gems.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, instantly finishes that upgrade at the cost of one Book of Building. This icon is only shown if you have at least one Book of Building.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, instantly finishes that upgrade at the cost of one Book of Everything. This icon is only shown if you have at least one Book of Everything but do not have a Book of Building.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, gives the player the option to assign a Builder's Apprentice to that Building. This icon only is only shown if you have unlocked Builder's Apprentice for 500 Gems.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, boosts all your Builders for 1 hour by a factor of ten, at the cost of one Builder Potion. This icon is only shown if you have at least one Builder Potion.
Tapping this icon, which is displayed only while an upgrade is in progress, cancels the current upgrade and refunds half of the upgrade cost.
Tapping this icon gears up the Cannon, if you have upgraded it enough to do so, have enough resources and a free Master Builder. If any Cannon is already geared up, this icon will not appear.
This icon informs you that the Cannon cannot be geared up because it is insufficiently upgraded. Tapping this icon will tell you the required level to gear up this Cannon. If any Cannon is already geared up, this icon will not appear.
This icon indicates that the Cannon is currently set to Normal mode, to attack units in the usual manner. Tapping this icon switches the Cannon to Burst mode. This icon only appears when the Cannon is geared up, and is in Normal mode.
This icon indicates that the Cannon is currently set to Burst mode, to attack units with burst shots. Tapping this icon switches the Cannon to Normal mode. This icon only appears when the Cannon is geared up, and is in Burst mode.

  • In the Single Player Campaign, the Cannon that appears in the "Payback" level is weaker than a normal level 1 Cannon, doing only 2 damage per second and only having 250 hitpoints. Oddly, that Cannon is also not considered a defensive building, since defense-targeting troops such as the Giant or Balloon would target the Goblin Hall if placed at the bottom of the level.
  • The Cannon is the only defense in the game that appears in all three villages: Home Village, Builder Base, and Clan Capital.
  • At Town Hall 17, all of the normal and geared up Cannons can be merged into Ricochet Cannons and the Multi-Gear Tower.

Summary

  • Cannons are the first defensive building in the Builder Base. They are unlocked at Builder Hall level 2 (they are fixed from their ruin).
  • Cannons are both cheap and quick to upgrade at lower levels.
  • Cannons can shoot any ground units, such as Raged Barbarians, but not any air units, such as Beta Minions or Baby Dragons.

Strategies

Defensive Strategies

  • Cannons have a high rate of fire and a decent range.
  • Cannons are very good point defenses that can deal massive damage to ground tanks, as long as they are protected.
  • You can defend with splash damaging defenses such as Multi Mortar, Crushers, and Roaster by placing them within range of Cannons.
  • Make sure Cannons are placed within range of Firecrackers, Archer Towers, or Air Bombs, because Cannons are unable to target air units.
  • Upgrade these structures first at lower levels, as they are one of your main ground defenses. They are also the cheapest and one of the most common defensive structure available, which makes them a good initial investment.

Offensive

  • Cannons are one of the few defensive structures that cannot attack air units. This makes them easy pickings for Beta Minions or Baby Dragons if they are not within range of Firecrackers, Archer Towers, Hidden Teslas, or Air Bombs.
    • The Beta Minions' Long Shot ability allows them to destroy a Cannon while out of range if there are enough and placed correctly.
  • Cannons are only able to damage one unit at a time. This makes it very easy to overwhelm them with large groups of Sneaky Archers or Raged Barbarians.
  • You can destroy a lone Cannon without any distraction buildings with three Sneaky Archers of relative level.

Upgrade Differences

  • In ruin, the Cannon is sunk into the ground within its wooden mount.
  • When initially constructed, it consists of a short dark metal barrel mounted on a crude four-legged wooden rotational mount made of multiple planks on top of a circular metal plate.
  • At level 2, the whole Cannon is bigger. The wood of rotational mount is now one whole piece instead of multiple wooden planks, the legs are rotated 45 degrees and receives wooden stabilizers tied to each legs with ropes.
  • At level 3, the barrel is bigger and receives two decorative latitudinal ridges, one midway along the barrel and one at the rear. The wood of rotational mount is replaced with metal and bottom circular metal plate becomes bigger. The platform's leg stabilizer is removed in favor of a larger and stouter main support platform with the rotational mount is fitted inside, receives riveted metal plates on the sides to anchor the barrel more firmly to the rotational mount, and the wooden legs are replaced with shorter metal legs with a rivet.
  • At level 4, the top of rotational mount is reverted to wood again. The main support is polished, the riveted metal plates and legs are removed for a stone foundation.
  • At level 5, the barrel is bigger and used a darker metal, the decorative ridges are thicker and trimmed to be more pointed, one more is added around the muzzle.
  • At level 6, the rotational mount is raised, and the wooden base and stone foundation are discarded for a taller white stone platform with a pair of gold plate is added to the right edge of the platform and its opposing side, and four short gold-trimmed wooden legs.
  • At level 7, the barrel's decorative middle ridges is made of gold and the bottom rotational mount has a straight edge. There are two additional gold plates perpendicular to the first pair and they are all wider, the side of them also receive a steel bolt with a gold top.
  • At level 8, the rear decorative ridge also becomes gold.
  • At level 9, the muzzle decorative ridge also becomes gold. The bottom rotational mount also becomes gold and four golden spikes are added to the top of the base no occupied by the side gold plate. The gold trimming on the legs is much bigger and the wooden leg is replaced with dark metal with cone-shaped spike.
  • At level 10, the barrel is entirely made of gold and bigger. The main body of the barrel receives triangular reinforcing plates and the decorative ridge are flattened to be a band, with a wider one added to its neck.
  • The appearances of the cannonball changes with the level of the Cannon.
    • When first constructed, the cannon shoots small, light grey color cannonballs.
    • At level 2, it shoots bigger cannonballs.
    • At level 4, the cannonballs becomes even bigger and becomes darker.
    • At level 6, it gains spikes around the cannonball.
    • At level 7, the cannonball becomes bigger again.
    • At level 8, the cannonball that is shot now glows.
    • At level 10, it is a dodecahedron with a small gap between each pentagonal pyramidal plates.

Summary

  • Cannons are among the first defensive buildings unlocked in the Clan Capital, available at Clan Capital level 1.
  • The Cannon is a single-target defense that deals moderate damage. Compared to Cannons in the Home Village and Builder Base, they have a much slower fire rate but have better damage per shot in return.
  • Cannons are cheap to upgrade with your clanmates at lower levels.
  • Cannons can shoot any ground unit, such as Super Barbarians and Sneaky Archers, but it can't shoot any air units, such as Flying Fortresses, Minion Hordes, etc.

Strategies

Defensive Strategy

  • Cannon, like the ones in the Home Village and Builder Base, have a high rate of fire and deals a decent amount of damage. They can be an ideal deterrent for ground troops that have low hitpoints such as Super Barbarians and Super Wizards. Also, their expertise is showcased for keeping Sneaky Archers at bay from chipping down defenses using their ability.
  • Cannons are feasible for keeping Super Giants from attacking other defenses.
  • Splash defenses which are weaker against high-health troops, such as Multi Mortars and Super Wizard Towers, can be defended by placing Cannons within their ranges.
  • To cover for their weakness, the air units, make sure to place them within range of Air Defenses and Spear Throwers. This is because they do not have the ability to target air units, making them vulnerable and useless.
  • Communicating with your clanmates to upgrade Cannons more often can be very promising. As Cannons can be upgraded with a cheap amount of Capital Gold, it can be beneficial to keep attackers away from the District Halls and the Capital Hall.
  • Putting Cannons two tiles behind walls can prevent Sneaky Archers from directly targeting them, allowing the Cannon to one-shot them in return when their ability wears off.

Offensive Strategy

  • Cannons cannot attack air units. This makes them easy pickings for Minion Hordes or Rocket Balloons if they are not within the range of defenses that can target air units such as Air Defenses or Spear Throwers.
  • Cannons are only able to damage one unit at a time. This makes it very easy to overwhelm them with large groups of troops such as Sneaky Archers or Skeletons from Skeleton Barrels. They can also be overwhelmed with higher health units such as Super Giants or Mountain Golems.
  • Strong solo troops such as Super Barbarians can destroy a Cannon even when surrounded by trash buildings if it has low support.

Upgrade Differences

  • Cannons undergo significant visual changes at levels 3, 4, and 5.
    • When initially rebuilt, it consists of a dark metal barrel mounted on a crude six-legged hexagonal wooden platform. Also, it is also surrounded by a few dark metal cannonballs.
    • At level 2, the middle banded part of the barrel gets covered with bolts and the barrel appears to be thicker than before, due to a visible addition of another metal band near the muzzle. It receives an elevation to its platform and the base receives a stone block foundation and wooden supports across each leg.
    • At level 3, the platform is more elevated. The base receives a reinforced step-like foundation made from stone and stone supports connect each stone block from below. Also, the wooden supports on the legs are replaced with pieces of metal. More cannonballs surround the cannon than before.
    • At level 4, the cannon's barrel is reinforced in white casings with gold trims. The entire barrel under the casings seems to gain a darker, crude shade of grey.
    • At level 5, the cannon's barrel becomes hexagonal in shape and the cannon itself increases in size. The middle part of the barrel gains large metal spikes. The legs that were made entirely of stone, become thicker, more solid slabs with some receiving golden trims, thereby making the entire foundation more reinforced. Even more cannonballs surround the cannon.
